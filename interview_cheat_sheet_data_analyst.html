Tool Choice

Pandas vs NumPy answer

Say this: Pandas is for labeled business data, cleaning, grouping, joining, and reporting. NumPy is for fast numeric arrays, vectorized math, and simulation. I use Pandas when structure matters and NumPy when computation speed matters. In real projects I often use both together.

What interviewers want

They want reasoning, not just definitions

  • Can you choose the right tool for the task?
  • Can you explain output in business language?
  • Can you connect math to action?

Dashboard

How to describe a dashboard structure

  1. Start with KPI cards.
  2. Add a trend chart for movement.
  3. Add category comparison for priority.
  4. Add distribution/outlier view for risk.
  5. Add relationship chart for root-cause clues.
  6. Finish with action notes.
Interview line: A dashboard is complete only when it shows the business state, the drivers, and the next action.
Answer template

How to answer any visualization question

1) State the question

What business decision are we trying to support?

2) Choose the chart

Pick the chart that best shows that specific pattern.

3) Explain why not others

Reject charts that would hide time, categories, spread, or relationships.

4) End with action

Say what the business should do next.

Strong sentence

One-line answer for most questions

Say this: I choose the chart based on the decision I want to support. If I need trend, I use line. If I need comparison, I use bar. If I need distribution or outliers, I use histogram or box plot. If I need relationships, I use scatter or heatmap.

One minute pitch

Short self-introduction for analyst interviews

I am a data analyst who focuses on turning raw data into business decisions. I use Pandas for cleaning and reporting, NumPy for fast numeric logic, and visualization tools like Matplotlib and Seaborn to communicate trends, comparisons, distributions, and relationships. My goal is not just to analyze data, but to explain what it means and what action should happen next.

Practice tip: Say this in your own words, then add one project example where you used a chart to uncover an insight.
